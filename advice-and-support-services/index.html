Advice and Support Services

Should you need advice or support there are a number of organisations available to help you. We have compiled a list below and provided phone numbers and email addresses where possible.


Coastline customer service numbers

Free-phone – 08082 027728

Local rate – 01209 200200

Text (non-urgent enquiries) – 07781 472887

Urgent repairs and advice 01209 200200 (24 hours a day, seven days a week).

Type talk: Dial 18001 before the number you require if you are deaf, hard of hearing or speech impaired

 

Cornwall Council Welfare Rights Team:  01872 324457 (benefits advice)

Welfare Rights Team, Adult Social Services, Children, Families and Adults Directorate, Cornwall Council,  1st Floor – East Wing, New County Hall, Treyew Road, Truro, TR1 3AY

Email: welfarerightsteam@cornwall.gov.uk

 

Citizens’ Advice

For debt enquiries text DEBT to 78866. For all other enquiries text ADVICE to 78866

Drop-in service for DEBT ADVICE ONLY. Camborne Office.

9.30 – 11.30 on Mondays. Camborne Community Centre, South Terrace, Camborne,TR14 8SU Please bring proof of income and proof of debts.

 

Age UK Advice Line for older people

Free-phone: 0800 055 6112 (8am – 7pm 365 days a year)

 

Carers UK Adviceline for people who care for others

Free-phone: 0808 808 7777 (Monday & Tuesday 10am to 4pm.)

email advice@carersuk.org

 

NHS services

Out of hours health advice: 111 (can also advise if you are not registered with a GP)

For help to find an NHS dentist: 0333 006 3300

Emergency dentist: 0333 405 0290. Alternatively, call.

 

Outlook South West (offer counselling and Cognitive Behavioural Therapy): 01208 871905

Email: enq@outlooksw.co.uk

Website: www.outlooksw.co.uk

 

Kerrier Community Mental Health Team: 01209 318300 (office hours)

Out of hours mental health services for Carrick, Kerrier and Penwith: 0845 230 3902

Cornwall Partnership NHS Foundation Trust, Head Office: 01208 834600 (office hours) Switchboard: 01208251300

Carew House, Beacon Technology Park, Dunmere Road, Bodmin, PL31 2QN

 

SANE

Charity that provide emotional support and information services.

  • SANE line: Available on 0300 304 7000 from 4.30pm to 10.30pm every evening.
  • Textcare: Allows you to arrange for messages of support at times that are right for you.
  • Support Forum: Available 24 hours a day to share your experiences with other members and give and receive mutual support.
  • Letters: You can write to us at SANE Services, St. Mark’s Studios, 14 Chillingworth Road, Islington, London N7 8QJ.
  • Facebook and Twitter: Social media communities are a good source of contact, support and sharing. You can see all the content even if you don’t have a Facebook or Twitter account yourself.

 

Samaritans

Samaritans are there to listen to anything that is upsetting you, including intrusive thoughts and difficult thoughts of suicide and self-harm.

  • National free-phone: 116 123
  • Email Samaritans: jo@samaritans.org
  • Cornwall branch: 01872 277 277 (local call charges apply)
  • Samaritans Truro

19 Treyew Road, Truro, TR1 2BY

Open from 9am to 9pm every day of the year. You don’t need to make an appointment.

 

The Silver Line

If you’re an older person (over the age of 55), the Silver Line is there 24 hours a day, 365 days a year to provide information, support and friendship. You can call them from anywhere in the UK on 0800 4 70 80 90 (freephone).

 

CALM

If you’re a man experiencing distressing thoughts and feelings, the Campaign Against Living Miserably (CALM) is there to support you. They’re open from 5pm–midnight, 365 days a year. Their national number is 0800 58 58 58, and they also have a webchat service if you’re not comfortable talking on the phone.
